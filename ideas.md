# Polygon Windows Website Design Brainstorm

## Design Philosophy Exploration

### Idea 1: Scandinavian Minimalism with Warm Materiality
**Probability: 0.08**

**Design Movement:** Scandinavian Functionalism meets Japanese Wabi-Sabi

**Core Principles:**
- Radical simplicity through constraint (fewer elements, more breathing room)
- Materiality as honesty (warm wood tones, soft greys, natural textures)
- Asymmetric balance (not centered, but carefully weighted)
- Silence as communication (white space speaks louder than words)

**Color Philosophy:**
The palette reflects natural materials and the passage of light through glass. Warm whites and soft beiges ground the design, while muted greens suggest the view through the window—nature framed. Deep charcoal provides anchor points without harshness. This creates a calming, almost meditative environment.

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Hero image bleeds to edges with minimal overlay
- Content flows in staggered columns (not centered grid)
- Large typography with generous line height creates rhythm
- Sections separated by breathing room, not dividers
- Product showcase uses asymmetric grid (3+1 layout)

**Signature Elements:**
1. Soft horizontal lines (representing window profiles) subtly dividing sections
2. Warm gradient overlays on imagery (from soft beige to transparent)
3. Minimal geometric icons with thin strokes

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Fade-in animations on scroll (no bounce, no attention-seeking)
- Parallax light reflections that suggest glass and refraction
- Hover states are understated (slight color shift, never scale)
- No aggressive CTAs—buttons blend with typography hierarchy

**Animation:**
- Entrance: Fade in over 800ms with slight upward drift (20px)
- Scroll: Parallax at 0.3x speed for hero images
- Hover: Subtle color transition (300ms ease-out)
- Loading: Minimal dot animation, never spinning

**Typography System:**
- Headline: Playfair Display (serif, elegant, editorial)
- Body: Inter (geometric sans, 16px base, 1.8 line height)
- Accent: Thin sans-serif for labels and specs
- Hierarchy: Size + weight, never all-caps

### Idea 2: Architectural Brutalism with Refined Restraint
**Probability: 0.07**

**Design Movement:** Contemporary Brutalism + Swiss Style Grid

**Core Principles:**
- Structural honesty (grid-based, visible system, no hidden complexity)
- Monochromatic foundation with strategic color accents
- Raw typography (large, bold, unadorned)
- Materiality through contrast (light vs. dark, smooth vs. textured)

**Color Philosophy:**
A strict palette of blacks, greys, and off-whites creates architectural rigor. The muted green appears as a single accent—perhaps in a thin line or small geometric shape—drawing the eye to key information. This approach feels engineered, precise, and trustworthy.

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Visible grid system (12-column, clearly defined)
- Sections stack vertically with defined gutters
- Typography scales dramatically (H1 might be 72px, body 14px)
- Product images presented in stark, geometric frames
- Technical specs displayed as raw data tables or diagrams

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin geometric dividers (1px rules in muted green)
2. Bold sans-serif typography in all-caps for emphasis
3. Stark product photography with minimal styling

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Sharp, quick interactions (200ms transitions)
- Hover states reveal grid or highlight structure
- No softness—interactions are decisive and clear
- CTAs are bold, geometric buttons with high contrast

**Animation:**
- Entrance: Slide in from left or right (400ms)
- Scroll: Staggered reveal of grid elements
- Hover: Color inversion or border highlight
- Loading: Geometric progress indicator

**Typography System:**
- Headline: Courier New or IBM Plex Mono (monospace, technical)
- Body: Helvetica Neue or similar (geometric, clean)
- Accent: All-caps sans-serif for labels
- Hierarchy: Size + case (uppercase for emphasis)

### Idea 3: Contemporary Editorial with Soft Luxury
**Probability: 0.09**

**Design Movement:** Modern Magazine Design + Luxury Minimalism

**Core Principles:**
- Narrative-driven layout (tells a story through visual flow)
- Sophisticated use of negative space (breathing room with purpose)
- Layered imagery (overlaps, transparency, depth)
- Emotional restraint (premium without ostentation)

**Color Philosophy:**
The palette is warm and inviting—soft beiges and warm greys create a sense of comfort, while the muted green introduces a natural, organic element. Deep charcoal text ensures readability. This feels like a high-end design magazine: refined, thoughtful, and never trying too hard.

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric two-column layouts (image + text, staggered)
- Large hero images with subtle text overlays
- Product sections use magazine-style layouts (image + callout)
- Typography-led sections with minimal imagery
- Diagonal or curved transitions between sections

**Signature Elements:**
1. Soft drop shadows and subtle blur effects
2. Layered imagery with transparency
3. Elegant serif typography for headlines
4. Minimal geometric shapes (circles, soft rectangles)

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Smooth, luxurious animations (600ms+ transitions)
- Hover states reveal additional information (tooltips, overlays)
- Subtle scale shifts on interaction (1.02x, never aggressive)
- CTAs integrated into narrative flow, not highlighted

**Animation:**
- Entrance: Fade in with slight scale (1.0 to 1.02)
- Scroll: Slow parallax (0.5x speed) for depth
- Hover: Subtle shadow enhancement, slight lift
- Loading: Elegant line animation or dot sequence

**Typography System:**
- Headline: Garamond or Crimson Text (serif, elegant, editorial)
- Body: Lato or Proxima Nova (warm sans-serif, approachable)
- Accent: Thin serif for captions
- Hierarchy: Size + color + weight

## Selected Approach: Scandinavian Minimalism with Warm Materiality

**Rationale:** This approach best embodies the brand's core values—quiet confidence, engineering precision, and emotional warmth. The asymmetric layout prevents the design from feeling cold or corporate, while the warm color palette creates a sense of calm and protection. The minimal aesthetic respects the user's intelligence and allows the product to speak for itself.

**Key Design Decisions:**
- Playfair Display for headlines (editorial, premium, timeless)
- Inter for body text (clean, readable, modern)
- Asymmetric layouts with generous white space
- Warm beige and grey palette with muted green accents
- Soft animations and subtle interactions
- Large hero imagery with minimal overlay
- Product showcase in asymmetric grid
- Horizontal lines subtly dividing sections
